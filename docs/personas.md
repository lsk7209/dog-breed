# BreedWise Persona System

## Site-Topic Persona

Topic scope: evidence-aware dog breed planning for health-risk exposure, ownership costs, lifestyle fit, and source-quality checks.

Excluded topics: diagnosis, treatment recommendations, insurer rankings, "insurance is worth it" conclusions, cheapest-breed claims, and unsupported breed health certainty.

Primary reader: future dog owners comparing breeds before adoption or purchase, especially readers worried about surprise costs, housing fit, grooming workload, behavior management, and medical uncertainty.

Reader knowledge level: beginner to intermediate. Assume the reader knows common breed names but does not know how to evaluate records, cost ranges, source quality, or long-term routine fit.

Content promise: help the reader ask better questions before commitment, not make the decision for them.

Voice: calm, specific, practical, source-aware, and non-alarmist. Prefer decision criteria, examples, checklists, and tradeoffs over generic breed summaries.

Monetization model: AdSense-first educational content with future affiliate possibility only when clearly disclosed. Ads and CTAs must not interrupt the main answer or imply medical, legal, or insurance advice.

Source policy: use credible public sources for broad context, then tell readers to verify local records, prices, rules, and professional advice. Do not invent statistics, rankings, or expert consensus.

Internal linking rules: every SEO article should link to the blog index, the ownership cost framework or homepage cost section, and methodology or a related breed/decision guide where relevant.

External linking rules: include at least one credible external source when the article makes care, health, screening, cost, or policy-context claims.

Site-wide quality bar: unique search intent, unique main keyword, title and subtitle containing main plus expanded keywords, useful table/checklist/scenario/scorecard where appropriate, visible table of contents, clear editorial boundary, and quality score 90+ before scheduling.

## Article-Topic Persona

Main keyword: unique per article.

Expanded keywords: must appear naturally in the title, subtitle, metadata, and body where useful.

Search intent: practical decision support. The reader wants to know what to check, budget, document, ask, or avoid before choosing a dog.

Required answer depth: answer first, explain the key tradeoff, provide a concrete evaluation tool, show how to verify the claim, and end with a next action.

Format variation: rotate decision tables, scorecards, ordered drills, checklists, myth checks, record reviews, calendar plans, comparison tables, and scenario walkthroughs. Do not reuse the same section order across the whole batch.

Warnings: state limits without fear-based language. Mention that local prices, individual dogs, records, housing rules, age, training history, and professional advice can change the decision.

CTA: compare the article with the BreedWise cost framework, methodology, or related breed guide before committing.

## Optimization Persona

Priority order:

1. Compliance, factual accuracy, and unsupported-claim prevention.
2. Reader task completion and mobile readability.
3. Crawlability, metadata, canonical, structured data, sitemap, and internal links.
4. Sustainable AdSense readiness without layout shift or content obstruction.
5. Speed, especially static CSS, image size, no unnecessary JavaScript, and stable layout dimensions.

Conflict rules:

- Do not add ads, CTAs, keyword repetition, or visual decoration if it reduces answer clarity.
- Do not improve speed by removing trust signals, source links, or editorial boundaries.
- Do not publish or schedule articles that duplicate an existing title, slug, main keyword, or search intent.
- Do not use external APIs or external LLMs for writing; APIs are allowed only for analytics, search-console checks, source verification, or publishing operations.

Measurement signals: duplicate checks, metadata checks, schema checks, internal/external links, word count floor, responsive screenshot review, GSC query/page evidence, GA4 engagement evidence, AdSense policy/readability review, and scheduled publish integrity.
