Methodology

Built to be slower, sourced, and harder to fake.

BreedWise avoids a single cheapest-breed ranking. The useful signal comes from separating clinical prevalence, US cost references, model assumptions, and review status.

Curate breed evidence

Start with breeds that have usable VetCompass coverage, then map source fields and limitations.

Attach cost references

Use US ownership surveys, industry reports, and claim references for cost bands where support exists.

Calculate exposure

Publish the formula behind cost-risk exposure, 5-year cost, fit scores, and discarded weak signals.

Review and disclose

Label reviewer status, affiliate relationships, and the UK-prevalence to US-cost limitation.

Trust model

Clear source boundaries are part of the product.

The site should earn trust by saying what it knows, where it learned it, and what it cannot conclude. That is especially important for pet health and insurance-adjacent content.

Information, not advice

BreedWise is intended for planning and research. It does not diagnose pets, recommend treatment, rank insurers, or decide whether insurance is worth it. Estimates depend on source coverage, location, age, individual health, provider pricing, and owner choices. Affiliate links may appear on tool pages and will be disclosed where used. This prototype has no live affiliate relationships and no named veterinary reviewer yet.
